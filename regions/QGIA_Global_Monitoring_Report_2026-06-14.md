## THEATER I: IRAN / MIDDLE EAST
### Operation Epic Fury — Day 107

#### 1. MoU Status: Imminent But Unconfirmed

The proposed Memorandum of Understanding is a **14-point framework** confirmed by Iranian FM Araghchi on June 13. Key publicly confirmed terms include:
- Immediate halt to fighting on all fronts, including Lebanon
- Lifting of the US naval blockade on Iranian ports
- Reopening of the Strait of Hormuz under Iranian arrangements within 30 days
- Release of frozen Iranian assets (estimated at $24 billion)
- 60-day negotiation window for nuclear issues and final agreement
- US pledge not to interfere in Iran's internal affairs
- Suspension of US oil and petrochemical sanctions

The MoU is a **two-stage structure**: Stage 1 ends the war and reopens Hormuz; Stage 2 (60-day follow-on) addresses nuclear program, further sanctions relief, and reconstruction (per Iranian semi-official outlet Mehr, citing a source close to the negotiating team). ISW assessed June 13 that "some terms of the agreement are at odds with stated US positions" — specifically on Hormuz (Iran insists it retains sovereignty and will charge for passage; US insists it will be "open to all").

**Signing probability estimate:** A senior US official Friday rated confidence at "80-85%." Pakistani PM Shehbaz Sharif stated the deal was "finalized" and readied an electronic signing event. ABCP composite places probability of MoU signature within 72 hours at **0.78**. Iranian Foreign Ministry's "coming days" language is the primary timing constraint.

#### 2. The Nuclear Fault Line

The central unresolved dispute: Iran holds **440.9 kg of uranium enriched to 60% purity** (IAEA-confirmed, one technical step below weapons-grade). Khamenei issued a directive **three days before** the deal's planned announcement **barring uranium transfer abroad**. An Iranian source to Reuters Sunday stated nuclear matters are "not part of the current arrangement." A US official simultaneously stated Iran "will agree in principle to dispose" of the stockpile.

This divergence is not noise — it is a structural incompatibility embedded in the MoU's ambiguity. The deal language (as reported by Times of Israel) is deliberately vague: the agreement "leads to" dismantling Iran's nuclear program rather than *requiring* it. The 60-day window is designed to paper over this gap, but it will reemerge as the primary breakdown vector in follow-on talks.

The US proposal as of early May required Iran to:
- Halt uranium enrichment for **12 years minimum**
- Surrender ~440 kg of enriched uranium
- Reopen Hormuz within 30 days post-signing

Iran's position has consistently been that enrichment is an NPT-guaranteed right and enriched material will not be exported. Zarif's April roadmap proposed a regional fuel-enrichment consortium under international oversight as a compromise — this remains the most viable long-term framework but is not in the current MoU.

- **Tier I (>25%):** MoU signed, preliminary uranium inspections agreed — probability **0.28**

The Lebanon ceasefire — extended 45 days from April 16 following US-brokered Washington talks — is formally separate from the US-Iran MoU but Iran has made Lebanon cessation a precondition of the broader deal.

Trump announced June 2 that Israel and Hezbollah had reached a mutual cessation agreement, with Hezbollah MP Hassan Fadlallah backing a "full ceasefire on all Lebanese territory." However, Israeli military operations have continued, citing Hezbollah projectile launches as ceasefire violations.

**Key divergence:** Iran's MoU readout includes Lebanon. US/Israeli position treats Lebanon as a parallel track. This creates a sequencing trap — Iran may demand visible Israeli restraint in Lebanon before finalizing MoU, while Israel uses Lebanon leverage to constrain the deal.

Netanyahu's posture remains the wild card. He was "caught between the US, Lebanon war, and Iran ceasefire," with electoral incentives to escalate (elections scheduled before October 27) and legal incentives to prolong crisis (corruption trial resumed April 9 after state of emergency lifted; Netanyahu has repeatedly sought delays citing "sensitive security and diplomatic factors"). #### 1. Battlefield Momentum Shift

Brigadier Biletsky (Ukraine's Third Army Corps) said in May that Russia is "worn out and unable to achieve significant breakthroughs" and identified a **critical six-month window** for Ukraine to seize battlefield initiative ahead of potential peace talks.

#### 2. Ukraine's "Logistics Lockdown" Strategy

Ukraine's most decisive innovation has been the escalating campaign against Russian rear logistics rather than frontal assault:

- May: Ukrainian drones targeted **180,000 objectives** — a 12.7% increase over April
- June 13: Ukrainian drone strike damaged the Volna maritime oil terminal in Krasnodar's Temryuk district
- June 12: Strikes on Volgograd oil processing facility
- May 30: Ballistic launcher and Tu-142 bombers destroyed at Taganrog airbase
- Attacks on Ilsky refinery (one of Russia's largest) and Novoshakhtinsky refinery
- Crimea fuel rationing in effect; most Sevastopol fuel stations running dry

Ukraine describes this as "long-range sanctions" — systematic degradation of Russia's ability to sustain forward-deployed forces.
