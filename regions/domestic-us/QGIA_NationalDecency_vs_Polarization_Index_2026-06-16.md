## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The quantifiable data on American prosocial behavior runs in direct and sustained opposition to the dominant narrative of national decline through political polarization. **76% of Americans gave money to charity in the past year. 63% volunteered their time. 67% performed a good deed for a stranger.** These figures are not historical artifacts of a more united era — they are from 2025 data collected during the period of maximum reported political polarization. The polarization is real. The decency is also real. They coexist, and the analytical failure is treating them as mutually exclusive. This document formalizes the **National Decency vs. Polarization Index** as a permanent ABCP domestic variable, establishing the evidential baseline and the predictive implications for midterm modeling.

They are simultaneously being more generous than they perceive themselves to be. This gap — between the story Americans tell about themselves and the behavior they actually exhibit — is one of the most politically significant findings in recent social science.

## II. THE TWO-LAYER MODEL — FORMALIZING THE ANALYTICAL DISTINCTION

The data establishes what the World Cup behavioral record confirmed empirically. There are two distinct layers of the American public, and they operate on different circuits:

### Layer 1: The Political Layer
Mediated. Abstract. Tribal. Governed by fear, group identity, and information environment. This is the layer that produces:
- Trump's 35% approval rating
- The +5.8% Democratic generic ballot lead
- 77% believing polarization has damaged American generosity
- Record partisan animosity scores in Pew research
- The electoral conditions for midterm seat flipping

**This layer is real, consequential, and the primary input for electoral forecasting.**

### Layer 2: The Interpersonal/Civic Layer
Immediate. Concrete. Governed by basic social norms, human presence, and the ambient default settings of a society that has — whatever its political pathologies — built deep prosocial infrastructure over two centuries. This is the layer that produces:
- 76% charitable giving
- 63% volunteering
- 67% good deeds for strangers
- Hotel receptionists driving tourists in the rain
- Kansas City deli owners giving free lunch
- USMNT fans welcoming Iranian players

**This layer is equally real, equally consequential, and systematically underweighted in political forecasting.**

### The Critical Analytical Point: These Layers Do Not Cancel Each Other

The dominant error in both pro-Trump and anti-Trump analytical frameworks is treating these layers as mutually exclusive. The pro-Trump frame argues that decency is real, therefore the political crisis is exaggerated. The anti-Trump frame argues that the political crisis is real, therefore decency claims are naive. Both are wrong. The layers coexist in the same people, activated by different contexts.

The person who:
- Voted for Trump in 2024 (Layer 1 activation: fear, grievance, tribal identity)
- Drove a foreign tourist to a stadium in the rain (Layer 2 activation: human presence, hospitality norm, basic decency)

...is not a hypocrite. They are a human being operating in two different registers, governed by two different sets of social cues. This is normal psychology. What is abnormal — and what the current political environment is producing — is the sustained activation of Layer 1 at the expense of Layer 2 through relentless mediated fear and grievance.

## III. THE VOLUNTEERING REBOUND — A LEADING INDICATOR

The Gallup data contains a detail that deserves its own analytical emphasis. Volunteering is **seven points higher** in 2025 than in 2021 [cite: Gallup, January 2026]. Volunteering for nonreligious organizations has reached **55% — the first majority-level reading in Gallup's history of measuring this metric.**

This is happening simultaneously with:
- Peak reported political polarization
- Near-record partisan animosity
- 70% reporting polarization has damaged their wellbeing
- A cost-of-living crisis reducing financial giving

The interpretation: as financial giving has softened under economic pressure, Americans are **substituting time for money.** They are not withdrawing from civic engagement — they are redirecting it. The form of generosity is changing; the impulse is not.

For midterm modeling, this is a leading indicator of civic activation potential. A population that is volunteering at majority levels — for the first time on record — has a latent civic infrastructure that can be channeled into electoral participation. Volunteering and voting draw from the same psychological reservoir: the belief that individual action has collective consequences. A population that believes this enough to give its Saturday to a food bank believes it enough to wait in a line to vote.

**ABCP probability adjustment:** Civic activation potential for midterm turnout should be revised upward by +4-6 points from baseline model. Current model is underweighting Layer 2 civic infrastructure as a turnout predictor.

## IV. THE PERCEPTION GAP — THE MOST POLITICALLY EXPLOITABLE VARIABLE

The single most actionable finding in this dataset is the gap between **perceived** and **actual** American generosity:

- **77%** believe polarization has made Americans less willing to give
- **76%** gave money to charity anyway

Americans believe their country is becoming less decent. It is not becoming less decent, by the behavioral metrics. The gap between the belief and the reality is politically exploitable — and it is currently being exploited exclusively by the fear-and-grievance architecture of the Trump political project, which benefits from Americans believing the country is in terminal decline and that only radical action (i.e., continued Trumpism) can arrest the decline.

The opposition has not effectively deployed the counter-narrative: *the country is more decent than you think it is, and you are part of the reason why.* This counter-narrative is both true (supported by data) and politically activating (it generates pride rather than shame, and pride is a better turnout motivator than fear in midterm electorates). The World Cup is generating this counter-narrative organically and at scale, without any opposition political operation having to manufacture it.

**This is the Hope Paradox mechanism expressed in quantitative terms:** hope is not just a feeling. It is a measurable behavioral baseline — 76% giving, 63% volunteering, 67% good deeds for strangers — that has been politically invisible because the dominant media frame, optimized for engagement through conflict, does not cover it.

## V. THE POLARIZATION-DECENCY DISSOCIATION — INTERNATIONAL COMPARISON

The dissociation between political polarization and prosocial behavior is not unique to the US, but the US version is particularly pronounced. Cross-national data from Gallup's World Happiness Report framework and the CAF World Giving Index consistently ranks the US among the top 10 most charitable nations globally — despite the US also ranking among the most politically polarized developed democracies.

The analytical implication: **political polarization and prosocial behavior are measuring different things.** Polarization measures the intensity of group-based animosity in the political domain. Prosocial behavior measures the ambient default settings of a society's interpersonal culture. These are driven by different variables — media environment, institutional trust, and political identity salience drive polarization; religious tradition, community infrastructure, wealth levels, and cultural norms drive prosocial behavior. The US has a particularly strong prosocial cultural tradition that appears to be robust even to severe political polarization.

This is consistent with the World Cup observation: the political environment is at peak toxicity, but the hotel receptionist drives the tourist in the rain anyway. The prosocial impulse is operating at a layer below the political layer, and it is not responding proportionally to political deterioration.

## VI. THE COUNTERWEIGHT — WHAT THE DATA DOES NOT SHOW

Registering the limitations with analytical rigor:

- **Trust in institutions** is declining sharply and has not recovered — this is a real and separate variable from interpersonal prosocial behavior, and low institutional trust does feed polarization
- **Hate crime data** remains elevated and is not captured by volunteering statistics — the racist/bigot population is real, present, and documented
- **Geographic variation** is significant — the ambient decency baseline is not uniformly distributed, and certain communities experience a very different ambient distribution than the median
- **The financial giving decline** is real — down 5 points from 2021 — and reflects genuine economic pressure that is itself a political variable
- **82% of Americans say seeing more generosity would make them more optimistic** [cite: Give Freely, 2024] — which means the perception of declining generosity is itself damaging optimism, even as actual generosity holds. The perception gap is not neutral; it has real behavioral consequences.
