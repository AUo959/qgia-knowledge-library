### Body Fat: The Physiologically Impossible Claim

The May 2026 physical report claimed **5% body fat**.

Clinical literature establishes what 5% body fat means for men (Harvard Medical School; peer-reviewed ranges):

- **2–5%**: Essential fat only — the absolute physiological survival floor; found only on competitive bodybuilders immediately pre-competition after months of extreme restriction and daily training
- **6–13%**: Elite athletes in active competition
- **14–24%**: Normal non-athlete range (age-dependent)
- **25–30%**: Overweight
- **Over 30%**: Clinically obese by body composition

For a **sedentary, 80-year-old male** at borderline-obese BMI, with chronic venous insufficiency and **no documented exercise program**, the clinical literature on sarcopenic obesity places the realistic body fat range at **30–37%**. The NIH-published clinical intervention ceiling for men over 70 is **37.4% total fat mass**.

**The 5% claim is off by a factor of six to seven.** This is not a rounding error. This is not spin. A living human being cannot function at 5% body fat while sedentary, overweight, and 80 years old. The claim is physiologically impossible and was placed in the document because no one in the White House press corps was expected to do the clinical math.

The most defensible clinical estimate for Trump's actual body fat, given all documented inputs: **30–37%** — consistent with clinical obesity by body composition regardless of the manipulated BMI figure.
