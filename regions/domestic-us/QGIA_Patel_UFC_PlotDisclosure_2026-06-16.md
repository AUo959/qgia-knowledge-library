## I. WHAT HAPPENED — THE OPERATIONAL RECORD

### The Plot

Five men are now in custody; investigators have identified a **network of 23 individuals** communicating in a Signal group chat who allegedly planned to:
- Detonate **explosive-laden drones** over the UFC arena on the White House lawn during Freedom 250 (June 14)
- Then have **snipers open fire** on "high value targets" in the crowd

The FBI became aware of the plot on **June 10** — four days before the event [cite: CNBC, June 16, 2026]. The Secret Service led the investigation from that point, working around the clock with FBI agents embedded as partners [cite: The Independent, June 16, 2026].

### The Plan That Was Agreed Upon

By the morning of June 15, the joint investigation was ongoing. Secret Service and FBI officials had **agreed on a coordinated plan:**
- Continue seeking additional arrests
- Unseal the case by **late Tuesday afternoon**
- Issue a **joint public statement** together

Approximately **ten suspects had not yet been arrested** and were still at large at the time of Patel's announcement [cite: MSNOW/NBC, June 15, 2026].

### What Patel Did

On the morning of June 15, Patel posted on X — before the agreed timeline, before all arrests were made, while the case remained sealed in court — announcing the FBI had disrupted a "multi-state operation" and that "allegedly planned attacks were halted immediately." He thanked the DOJ and "law enforcement partners" [cite: BBC, June 16, 2026].

*"We all woke up this morning to see this on Twitter,"* said one administration official, speaking confidentially [cite: MSNOW, June 15, 2026].

### The Secret Service Response

Secret Service Deputy Director Matt Quinn addressed the situation at a press conference without using Patel's name:

> *"I'll tell you a phrase I learned early in my career in the New York field office and that's 'Don't choke on your own smoke.'"*

> *"I'll tell you the Secret Service led that investigation from the beginning. I'll tell you that case is ongoing. In order to maintain the integrity of the investigation and the security plan, we chose not to leak it."* [cite: BBC / The Independent, June 16, 2026]

Secret Service Director Sean Curran issued a separate statement affirming the agency "collaborated closely with the FBI throughout this investigation" and indicated the Secret Service's formal comments on case details would be provided **through court filings** — a deliberate contrast to Patel's social media announcement [cite: CNBC, June 16, 2026].

### Trump's Response

Asked about the alleged plot at the G7 summit in France on Tuesday, Trump stated: **"I haven't heard about it."** [cite: BBC, June 16, 2026]

Vice President Vance, by contrast, defended Patel's disclosure on Fox News, citing the "scale of the planned attack" and describing it as a "coordinated planned terrorist plot" with radical left-wing funding networks [cite: Le Monde, June 16, 2026].
