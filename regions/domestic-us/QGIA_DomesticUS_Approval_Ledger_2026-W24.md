### Virginia Redistricting
Virginia voters decided on a congressional redistricting referendum (April 2026) that, if passed, would redraw the map to yield 8 Safe D / 2 Lean D / 1 Safe R districts out of 11 total seats — a potential net Democratic gain of **4–5 seats** from Virginia alone. This factor was absent from the initial projection and materially shifts the House seat ceiling upward. Resolution of the referendum outcome required before this factor can be fully incorporated into the probability table.

### Texas Senate — Paxton Nominee Effect
Ken Paxton defeated John Cornyn in the May 26, 2026 Republican Senate primary run-off (64% vs. 36%). Paxton is broadly assessed as a weaker general election candidate than Cornyn. Democrat James Talarico faces Paxton in November. Texas was not previously rated competitive; Paxton's nomination makes it a **reach Democratic target** in a D+8 environment. No major forecaster has yet upgraded Texas to Toss-Up, but QGIA models this as a latent structural improvement to the Democratic Senate path.
