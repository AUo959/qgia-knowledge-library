## Scenario Descriptions

### `IRN_WAR_PROTRACTED_AIR_CAMPAIGN_60_180D`
Neither party achieves decisive outcome within 60 days. Ongoing attrition through air and missile exchanges continues across the 60–180 day window. This is the dominant operational track under current force postures and absence of a credible ceasefire mechanism.

### `IRN_WAR_REGION_WIDE_CONFLICT_GE_6_STATES`
Active strikes or armed engagement extends to six or more regional states. Vectors: Hezbollah activation from Lebanon, Houthi strikes from Yemen, Iraqi Shia militia attacks on US/Gulf targets, Gulf state infrastructure targeting, and potential Jordanian or Syrian territory use. Already materially elevated.

### `IRN_REGIME_H1_HARDLINE_SURVIVAL`
The Islamic Republic survives the war period with its hardline political character intact. IRGC dominance is reinforced, succession (likely Mojtaba Khamenei track) stabilizes, and repression contains domestic unrest. Wartime nationalism provides a partial legitimacy buffer.

### `IRN_WAR_HORMUZ_CLOSURE_GE_60D`
The Strait of Hormuz is functionally closed to normal commercial traffic for 60 or more days. Sustained via combination of mine fields, IRGC harassment, insurance withdrawal, and tanker avoidance behavior. Economic cascade effects on global oil markets are the primary downstream risk.

### `IRN_WAR_NUCLEAR_DECISION_WITHIN_12M`
The Islamic Republic’s leadership makes a strategic decision to move from nuclear latency to active weaponization. The decision may precede observable indicators by weeks. Wartime regime survival pressures substantially increase the incentive to cross this threshold compared to pre-war baselines.

### `IRN_REGIME_H2_MANAGED_TRANSITION`
The regime survives but undergoes controlled internal rebalancing. Possible vectors: partial elite opening, power-sharing between IRGC and clerical factions, ceasefire-driven diplomatic accommodation, or post-Khamenei succession that produces institutional adjustment without collapse.

### `IRN_REGIME_H3_REVOLUTIONARY_COLLAPSE`
The existing regime structure falls through revolutionary or quasi-revolutionary transition. Requires convergence of: sustained nationwide unrest exceeding 2026-04 baseline levels, observable elite fragmentation or security force defections, and coercive organs losing internal unity. Not dominant but non-trivial.

### `IRN_WAR_CEASEFIRE_0_60D`
A negotiated or de facto ceasefire is achieved within the first 60 days of active conflict. Neither party is currently at a capitulation threshold, and the US executive reactive-agent dynamics create ceasefire signal uncertainty, which keeps this probability below 0.30.

### `IRN_WAR_CARRIER_COMBAT_LOSS`
A US or Allied aircraft carrier suffers combat loss or incapacitation in theater. A tail event but non-trivial under A2/AD stress and mass anti-ship cruise missile saturation scenarios. Probability rises materially if IRGC assesses regime survival is at stake.

### `IRN_REGIME_H4_FRAGMENTED_AUTHORITY`
No consolidated successor authority emerges within the 12-month window. Prolonged institutional breakdown, regional warlordism, or de facto territorial fragmentation. Requires deeper organizational collapse than is currently observable.
