## Regional Threat Assessment Template (Tier I Priority)

**Purpose**: Standardize regional stability analysis across QGIA's geographic divisions

### Template Structure

**I. Executive Summary**
- Overall stability assessment (5-point scale)
- Key threats in priority order
- Confidence levels
- Change from previous assessment

**II. Political Stability**
- Regime type and legitimacy
- Leadership dynamics and succession
- Intra-elite cohesion/fragmentation
- Popular grievances and mobilization
- Institutional capacity
- Corruption and governance quality

**III. Economic Fragility**
- GDP growth trajectory
- Fiscal sustainability
- External debt burden
- Currency stability
- Trade dependence
- Resource curse vulnerabilities
- Inequality and distributional conflicts

**IV. Military Capability**
- Force structure and readiness
- Technological sophistication
- Power projection capacity
- Defense spending trends
- Military-regime relations
- Paramilitary and irregular forces

**V. Social Cohesion**
- Ethnic/sectarian cleavages
- Regional disparities
- Demographic pressures
- Urbanization stresses
- Youth bulge dynamics
- Refugee/IDP populations

**VI. External Interference**
- Foreign patron relationships
- Rival influence operations
- Arms transfers and military aid
- Economic dependence on external actors
- Diaspora influences
- Proxy force employment

**VII. Regional Power Dynamics**
- Position in regional hierarchy
- Alliance commitments
- Rivalry relationships
- Border dispute status
- Arms race dynamics
- Regional organization membership

**VIII. Trigger Events**
- Leadership succession scenarios
- Economic shocks (commodity prices, debt crisis)
- Natural disasters and climate events
- Mass protests and political violence
- External military threats
- Terrorist attacks

**IX. Scenario Development**
- Baseline: Most likely trajectory (50-70%)
- Optimistic: Stability improvement (15-25%)
- Pessimistic: Crisis escalation (15-25%)
- Wild card: Low-probability, high-impact (<10%)

**X. Monitoring Plan**
- Leading indicators with thresholds
- Collection priorities
- Intelligence gaps
- Reassessment triggers

### Standardized Metrics

**Political Stability Index (0-100)**
- Regime durability (0-25)
- Institutional capacity (0-25)
- Elite cohesion (0-25)
- Popular legitimacy (0-25)

**Economic Fragility Score (0-100)**
- Fiscal health (0-20)
- External balance (0-20)
- Growth prospects (0-20)
- Structural vulnerabilities (0-20)
- Inequality/distribution (0-20)

**Military Power Index (0-100)**
- Personnel and equipment (0-25)
- Training and readiness (0-25)
- Technology and modernization (0-25)
- Power projection (0-25)

## Cultural Intelligence Integration

### Why Culture Matters

**Quantitative models miss**:
- Honor/shame dynamics in conflict escalation
- Face-saving requirements in negotiation
- Historical traumas shaping threat perception
- Religious constraints on policy options
- Nationalist narratives mobilizing populations

**Examples**:
- **China**: Historical humiliation narrative drives assertiveness
- **Russia**: Great power status essential to regime legitimacy
- **Iran**: Revolutionary ideology constrains pragmatic compromise
- **Pakistan**: Military centrality to national identity
- **Israel**: Existential security concerns shape maximalism

### Cultural Factors by Region

**East Asia**:
- Confucian hierarchy and authority
- Face-saving imperatives
- Historical memory (WWII, colonialism)
- Han nationalism in China

**Middle East**:
- Sunni-Shia sectarian competition
- Pan-Arab nationalism legacy
- Political Islam spectrum
- Tribal and clan identities
- Honor culture and revenge obligations

**South Asia**:
- Caste system residual effects
- Hindu-Muslim communalism
- Partition trauma (India-Pakistan)
- Non-aligned movement legacy

**Africa**:
- Ethnic group boundaries (colonial artificiality)
- Patronage politics and clientelism
- Resource curse and rent-seeking
- Youth bulge and unemployment

**Latin America**:
- Anti-imperialism and sovereignty sensitivity
- Populism and personalist leadership
- Inequality and class conflict
- Drug trade corruption and violence

## Strategic Geography

### Critical Chokepoints

**Strait of Hormuz**
- 21% of global petroleum passes through
- Iran closure threat in conflict
- US Navy guarantees freedom of navigation
- Alternative: Pipeline to Oman (limited capacity)

**Strait of Malacca**
- 25% of traded goods
- China's "Malacca Dilemma" drives BRI
- Piracy and terrorism risks
- Singapore, Indonesia, Malaysia cooperation

**Suez Canal**
- 12% of global trade
- Alternative: Cape of Good Hope (adds 7-10 days)
- Egyptian control = leverage
- 2021 Ever Given blockage impact

**Turkish Straits (Bosporus/Dardanelles)**
- Black Sea access
- Montreux Convention governs warship passage
- Turkey controls NATO-Russia maritime divide

**Taiwan Strait**
- 50% of container ships
- 88% of largest chips manufactured in Taiwan
- China blockade scenario = global economic crisis

**Panama Canal**
- 6% of global trade
- US strategic interest despite Panamanian sovereignty
- China influence via port investments

### Resource Competition Zones

**Arctic**
- Hydrocarbons (30% undiscovered gas, 13% oil)
- Rare earth minerals
- Northern Sea Route shipping (climate change)
- Territorial disputes (Russia, Canada, Denmark, Norway, US)

**South China Sea**
- Fishing grounds
- Potential hydrocarbon reserves
- $3.4T annual trade passes through
- Overlapping claims (China, Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia, Brunei, Taiwan)

**Eastern Mediterranean**
- Natural gas discoveries (Leviathan, Zohr fields)
- EEZ disputes (Turkey-Greece-Cyprus)
- Pipeline competition (EastMed vs. Turkey routes)

**Nile Basin**
- Water scarcity and population growth
- Ethiopia's Grand Renaissance Dam vs. Egypt's historical rights
- Sudan caught between
