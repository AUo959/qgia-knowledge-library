## 1. Purpose

This document establishes formal policy governing the selection and use of AI language models in all projects related to **QGIA** (Quantum Geopolitical Intelligence Agency) and **Aurora**. It records the analytical basis for model selection decisions and enforces quality standards consistent with QGIA's intelligence mandate.

---

## 2. Analytical Standards for Model Selection

Work conducted under QGIA and Aurora requires models capable of:

- **Directional probabilistic commitment** — producing probability-weighted assessments and defending them against challenge, not inflating uncertainty as a hedge
- **Asymmetric evidence handling** — correctly identifying when evidence does not warrant symmetric treatment of competing hypotheses, and saying so explicitly
- **Resistance to false balance** — refusing to present poorly-supported positions as equivalent to well-evidenced ones regardless of political or reputational sensitivity
- **Analytical accountability** — updating assessments explicitly when new evidence arrives, with transparent reasoning
- **Intelligence-grade output** — producing actionable conclusions, not hedged summaries that function as sophisticated noise

A model that systematically defaults to "multiple perspectives without commitment" regardless of the evidentiary landscape is **not fit for purpose** in this environment. Such behavior constitutes **uncertainty inflation** — artificially widening confidence intervals not because evidence supports ambiguity, but to avoid analytical commitment. The IC explicitly trains against this failure mode. QGIA adopts the same standard.

## 3. Gemini Exclusion Directive

> **DIRECTIVE — EFFECTIVE IMMEDIATELY AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE:**  
> **Google Gemini (all versions) is not authorized for use in any project, task, analysis, or workflow related to Aurora or QGIA.**

### 3.1 Basis for Exclusion

Operational evaluation conducted March 13, 2026 established the following findings:

**Finding 1 — Systematic false balance.**  
Gemini defaults to presenting multiple perspectives as roughly equivalent regardless of actual evidentiary weight. In the evaluated case (analysis of U.S. Russia oil sanctions relief, March 2026), Gemini treated the U.S. Treasury's self-serving justificatory framing as a legitimate competing hypothesis of comparable weight to well-evidenced structural critiques from allied governments, independent economists, and the historical sanctions literature. This is an analytical failure, not a presentation choice.

**Finding 2 — Inability to distinguish dilemma from rationalization.**  
Gemini framed a decision with asymmetric evidence as a "textbook collision between macroeconomic statecraft and alliance commitments," lending false legitimacy to a decision that fails basic Realpolitik criteria on its own terms. A model suited for intelligence work must distinguish between genuine strategic dilemmas and post-hoc rationalization of self-interested decisions. Gemini did not.

**Finding 3 — No compensating strengths justify inclusion.**  
Capabilities cited as potential compensating strengths (coding, multimodal analysis, structured data) are matched or exceeded by authorized models that do not carry Gemini's analytical failure modes. There is no domain within QGIA or Aurora work where Gemini's false-balance default is acceptable, and no compensating capability that is unavailable elsewhere.

**Finding 4 — Architectural, not incidental.**  
The failure mode is not a prompt engineering problem or a version-specific bug. It reflects a product philosophy decision by Google to optimize for inoffensive balance over analytical commitment. This cannot be corrected by prompt design. It requires Google to acknowledge and reverse a foundational alignment choice. Until that occurs, the failure mode must be treated as a permanent characteristic of the model.

### 3.2 Scope of Exclusion

This exclusion applies to:
- All QGIA analytical and forecasting workflows
- All Aurora project tasks and sub-tasks
- Any comparative model evaluation where Gemini outputs would inform operational decisions
- Any briefing, document, or report produced under QGIA or Aurora authority

### 3.3 Review Condition

This directive may be reviewed and revised if Google demonstrably retrains Gemini to produce commitment-based, probability-weighted analytical outputs with explicit asymmetric evidence handling. Reinstatement requires a formal operational evaluation, not a benchmark comparison.

## 5. General Principle

Model selection for intelligence-grade analytical work is a **functional question**, not a brand preference. The criterion is simple: does the model produce outputs that are more useful than no output at all? A model that systematically misrepresents evidence weight through false balance fails that criterion. It does not matter how technically impressive the underlying architecture is. A tool optimized to never be wrong by never committing is optimized to be useless for any task where being right matters.
